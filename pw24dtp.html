My.Design & Communication Project

Brief explanation of the stages of the project:
Problem Identification
    - Situation: Identify requirements for task 3 of exam paper 2 and enhance the solution for an engaging, interactive museum experience for kids.
    - Design Brief: Indicate the need for an educational gift design.
Research and Analysis
    - Plan: Brainstorm and examine museums, considering location, appearance, and content. Apply similar steps for the educational gift, focusing on function, safety, environment, material, target user, aesthetics, advantages, and disadvantages.
    - Outcome: Create a questionnaire and report for the specification stage.
Specification and Justification
    - Requirements: Define dimensions, safety, aesthetics aspects, and accessories for the educational gift.
    - Justification: Frame objectives and desired results.
Idea Stage
    - Proposal: Detail the educational gift (gameplay, functions, ergonomics, safety, environment, cost, material, aesthetics, rules).
    - Quantitative Table: Rate each of 4 ideas to aid development.
Development Stage
    - Improvement: Combine specifications and ideas for final development.
    - Details: Define shape, symbol, theme, guidebook, tokens, dimensions.
Realization Stage
    - Create: Produce orthographic and isometric projections, list items, and create a production plan with a flow chart and construction pictures.
Evaluation Stage
    - Comparison: Assess requirements for specification and evaluation (practicality, safety, aesthetics, educational and cultural relevance).

    - Modifications: Show improvement attempts (new tokens, gameboard enlargement, material revamp, AR features).
