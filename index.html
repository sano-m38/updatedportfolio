my.Fitness

I used to sit for hours, hunched over my laptop, the pressure mounting in my lower back. It wasn’t the worst pain, but it was persistent, gnawing at my focus, distracting me from my studies.

A friend casually mentioned the gym, specifically strength training, as a solution. The gym felt intimidating, a place for people chasing muscle mass, not for someone like me who just wanted relief from back pain and more focus to study.

But I decided to give it a shot. The first few sessions were humbling. I had no idea what I was doing, but slowly, I noticed changes not just in my back but in how I approached everything.

My ability to sit and concentrate grew exponentially. The gym became my therapy. It wasn’t just about the pain anymore as it became a mental cleanse, a time away from books and screens. When I returned to my studies, my mind was sharper, more focused, and free from the nagging discomfort that had once held me back. It was ironic, in a way.

The very thing I’d avoided for so long turned out to be the key to not only managing my pain but also unlocking a new level of productivity in my academic life.
